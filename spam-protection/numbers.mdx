## 🔄 Workflow

The spam protection system follows a comprehensive monitoring and management workflow:


 **Step-by-Step Process**

1. **🆕 Number Addition**: Add phone numbers to the protection system
2. **✅ Initial Verification**: System verifies number validity and carrier compatibility
3. **📋 Carrier Registration**: Number is registered with relevant carriers and compliance databases

5. **⚠️ Alert System**: Immediate notifications for status changes or flags
6. **🔧 Remediation**: Automated and manual actions to resolve issues
7. **🔄 Renewal Management**: Automatic renewal of protection services

#### 🚨 Option 1: Flag by 1 Carrier (Most Sensitive)

**Example:** Your number is flagged the moment any carrier (AT&T, T-Mobile, Verizon, etc.) reports it as spam.

**Pros:**
- ✅ Fastest possible detection—instant flagging
- ✅ Maximum caution for strict compliance
- ✅ Good for protecting your brand reputation

**Cons:**
- ⚠️ May result in more false positives
- ⚠️ Might flag due to isolated carrier mistakes

**Recommended For:**
- Businesses prioritizing reputation above all
- Low-volume or sensitive callers
- Compliance-heavy industries

---

#### ⭐ Option 2: Flag by 2 Carriers (Recommended - Default)

**Example:** Your number is only marked "Flagged" when both AT&T AND T-Mobile (or any 2 carriers) report it as spam

**Pros:**
- ✅ Reduces false positives significantly
- ✅ More accurate spam detection
- ✅ Balanced approach for most businesses
- ✅ Set as default for optimal results

**Cons:**
- ⚠️ Slightly slower detection than Option 1

**Recommended For:**
- Most businesses (Default choice)
- Companies with moderate call volumes
- Businesses wanting balance between accuracy and speed

---

#### 🛡️ Option 3: Flag by 3 Carriers (Least Sensitive)

**Example:** Your number is only flagged when AT&T, T-Mobile, AND Verizon (or any 3 carriers) all report it as spam

**Pros:**
- ✅ Almost no false positives
- ✅ Very high confidence in spam detection
- ✅ Won't trigger on isolated incidents

**Cons:**
- ⚠️ May detect spam issues later
- ⚠️ Your number might already have reputation damage before flagging

**Recommended For:**
- High-volume call centers
- Businesses with aggressive calling patterns
- Companies that accept some spam risk for fewer interruptions

# ⏳ How Phone Number Flagging & Verification Works

If a number shows as flagged today, that doesn’t mean it’s flagged forever. The status of your numbers is dynamic and can change daily, as we run these checks five times a week. This means:

- A **flagged** number today could become **clean** tomorrow.
- A **clean** number today might be **flagged** by the next check.

When you **purchase a phone number**, it may initially appear as clean. However, if carrier or device data changes and the number is subsequently flagged, its status will automatically update to **Unverified**. Once conditions improve and the number is no longer flagged, its status will update back to **Verified**.

**Key Points:**
- Each number is checked **once every 24 hours** (one time per day), so there is a natural window between checks where a number's status can change. For example, a number could be clean at the daily check but become temporarily flagged within the next 24 hours before the next scan.
- Our remediation team actively monitors numbers every day to ensure you are aware of any status changes.
- Checks are performed using **real-life devices**, giving you results based on what carriers and devices actually show in practical conditions.

> 🧪 **Note:** Continuous, round-the-clock testing is expensive to operate at scale. That's why your numbers receive standard periodic checks which are sufficient for most large call centers and businesses. If you require more frequent, tighter monitoring, you have the option to purchase additional checks for a specific number.

### 📋 Numnbers Flagging and Verification process

> **Note:** The spam status of a number is not static—statuses are dynamic and can change daily.

#### How Spam Status Updates Work:

- **Daily Changes:**  
  If a number shows as *flagged* today, that doesn’t mean it’s flagged forever. The status of numbers can change day by day, as we run our reputation checks five days a week.  
  - A number flagged today could be clean tomorrow.
  - A number clean today could become flagged in a future check.

- **Upon Purchase:**  
  When you first purchase a phone number, it may look *clean* at the start. However, if device or carrier data changes and the number later becomes flagged, the system will update the status to **Unverified**. Once the number is observed as clean again, its status will return to **Verified**.

- **Monitoring Frequency:**  
  - Each number is checked automatically once every 24 hours (once per day).
  - There is always a window of time between checks—meaning a number's reputation could change between scheduled tests. For example: if a number is clean at this morning’s check, but then gets flagged later today, it will only show as flagged after the next system run.

- **Testing Quality:**  
  Our tests use real-life hardware and real carrier lookups so your dashboard reflects the true, real-world status.

#### What If You Need Tighter Tracking?

- The default setup is single (1x) check per number per day, due to the cost and resource requirements of full device testing.
- If you require more frequent checks (e.g., for mission-critical numbers), you can purchase additional monitoring—contact support for details.
- Most large call centers find the daily, per-number process to be reliable for ongoing brand protection.

#### Our Remediation Team:

- We review flags and status changes every day.
- If your number is flagged unexpectedly, our team can help provide next steps for remediation or escalation.

> **Tip:** For best results, check and review your numbers at least weekly, and respond promptly to any changes in spam status.
